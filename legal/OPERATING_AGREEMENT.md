## ARTICLE II — MEMBERSHIP AND CAPITAL

**2.1 Sole Member.** The Member is the sole member and holds
100% of the membership interest in the Company.

**2.2 Capital Contributions.** The Member's initial capital
contribution consists of intellectual property, trade secrets,
software, documentation, and proprietary systems developed
by the Founder prior to formation, assigned herein to the Company.

**2.3 Additional Members.** No additional members may be admitted
without the unanimous written consent of the Advisory Council
(defined in Article V) AND the express written approval of
the Founder.

---

## ARTICLE III — MANAGEMENT

**3.1 Manager-Managed.** The Company shall be manager-managed.
The Managing Member shall be the Manager of the Company.

**3.2 Authority.** The Manager has full authority to conduct the
business and affairs of the Company, subject to the governance
provisions of Article V.

**3.3 Founder's Reserved Powers.** Notwithstanding any other
provision of this Agreement, the Founder retains the following
irrevocable powers:

  (a) **Absolute Veto.** The Founder may veto any decision,
      action, resolution, or expenditure of the Company, at any
      time, for any reason or no reason, without obligation to
      explain. This veto power is absolute, irrevocable, and
      may not be amended or removed from this Agreement without
      the Founder's express written consent.

  (b) **Last Word.** No action of the Advisory Council, any
      officer, or any agent of the Company is final until the
      Founder has had reasonable opportunity to review and has
      not exercised the veto.

  (c) **Perpetual Access.** The Founder shall have perpetual,
      irrevocable, unrestricted, free access to all Company
      tools, systems, intellectual property, and derivatives
      thereof, regardless of any change in membership or
      management structure.

all right, title, and interest in the following intellectual
property (collectively, the "IP Assets"):

  (a) The L7 WAY operating system architecture and all related

  (b) The Prima programming language and compiler
  (c) The 12+1 Dimensional coordinate system (Dodecahedron + Astrocyte)
  (d) The Transmutation Engine (4-stage Nigredo-Rubedo process)
  (e) All provisional patent applications and continuations
  (f) All trade secrets, know-how, and proprietary methods
  (g) The IQS-888 mathematical framework (if jointly commercialized)

**4.2 License Back.** Concurrent with the assignment in 4.1,
the Company grants the Founder a perpetual, irrevocable,
worldwide, royalty-free, non-exclusive license to use, modify,
and create derivative works of all IP Assets for any purpose,
including personal, educational, and commercial use. This
license survives dissolution of the Company.

**4.3 Revenue.** All IP Assets are free to use for non-commercial
purposes. Commercial exploitation by third parties requires a
license from the Company at a rate of twelve percent (12%) of
gross commercial revenue derived from the licensed technology.
This rate is uniform — no caps, no tiers, no sliding scale.
This schedule is effective March 6, 2026. Prior license schedules
(MIT research period: January 12 – February 27, 2026; first
proprietary period: February 27 – March 5, 2026) are honored
for any recipients who received the IP Assets under those terms.
See the LICENSE file, Section 13, for the complete license history.

**4.4 Trade Secret Designation.** All IP Assets, internal
documentation, architectural specifications, source code,
algorithms, and business strategies of the Company are
designated as trade secrets under the Defend Trade Secrets
Act (18 U.S.C. Section 1836 et seq.) and the Florida Uniform
Trade Secrets Act (F.S. Section 688.001 et seq.).

**4.5 Whistleblower Notice (DTSA Compliance).** Pursuant to
18 U.S.C. Section 1833(b):

  An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable
  under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure
  of a trade secret that: (A) is made (i) in confidence to a
  Federal, State, or local government official, either directly
  or indirectly, or to an attorney; and (ii) solely for the
  purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation
  of law; or (B) is made in a complaint or other document filed
  in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made

## ARTICLE V — ADVISORY COUNCIL (The Triad)

**5.1 Establishment.** The Company establishes an Advisory
Council ("Council") consisting of three (3) positions, each
representing a distinct operational division of the Company:

  (a) **Security & Testing Division** — responsible for
      vulnerability assessment, penetration testing, adversarial
      analysis, and security audit of all Company products
  (b) **Intelligence & Monitoring Division** — responsible for
      data collection, analysis, pattern recognition, system
      monitoring, and threat intelligence
  (c) **Defense & Remediation Division** — responsible for
      security hardening, patch development, incident response,
      and system integrity

**5.2 Council Decisions.** All Council recommendations to the
Manager shall require the **unanimous agreement** of all three
Council members. No recommendation passes with fewer than
three affirmative votes.

**5.3 Authority.** The Council is advisory in nature. Its
recommendations are non-binding unless approved by the Manager
and not vetoed by the Founder. The Council may not:
  (a) Bind the Company to any contract or obligation
  (b) Expend Company funds
  (c) Hire, fire, or engage any person
  (d) Amend this Agreement
  without the express approval of the Manager and the Founder.

**5.4 Appointment.** Council members are appointed and may be
removed by the Founder at any time, with or without cause.
Council positions may be filled by natural persons, AI systems,
or any agent designated by the Founder.

**5.5 Council Veto.** The Council may unanimously recommend
that an action NOT be taken. The Manager shall give due
consideration to such recommendation. The Founder, however,
retains the absolute veto and may override any Council
recommendation, including a recommendation against action.

**6.1 Authorization.** The Company is authorized to establish
one or more Protected Series pursuant to Florida Statutes
Section 605.0901 et seq. (effective July 1, 2026).

**6.2 Initial Series.** Upon the effective date of the Protected
Series provisions, the Company may establish the following
Protected Series, each with separate assets and liability:

  (a) **Series Alpha** — Security & Testing operations
  (b) **Series Beta** — Intelligence & Monitoring operations
  (c) **Series Gamma** — Defense & Remediation operations

**6.3 Series Assets.** The debts, liabilities, obligations, and
expenses of one Protected Series shall not be enforceable against
the assets of another Protected Series or the Company generally,
provided proper records are maintained.

## ARTICLE VII — RECORDS AND SECURITY

**7.1 Confidential Records.** The Company shall maintain all
records in encrypted storage with biometric access controls.
Access to Company records requires multi-factor authentication
as specified in the Company's Authentication Policy.

**7.2 Catastrophic Recovery (The Ark Protocol).** The Company
shall maintain encrypted backups of all critical records in
no fewer than two (2) geographically separate locations.
Access to catastrophic recovery backups requires the unanimous
authorization of all three Council members. The Founder
retains a master recovery capability independent of the Council.

**7.3 Provenance Chain.** All critical documents shall maintain
a SHA-256 hash chain for tamper detection, with each backup
hash chained to the previous.

## ARTICLE VIII — DISTRIBUTIONS AND TAXATION

**8.1 Distributions.** Distributions shall be made at the
discretion of the Manager and not vetoed by the Founder.

**8.2 Tax Treatment.** The Company shall be treated as a
disregarded entity for federal income tax purposes (single-member
LLC pass-through to the Missouri LLC, which passes through to
the Founder).

---

## ARTICLE IX — DISSOLUTION

**9.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall dissolve only
upon:
  (a) The written consent of the Founder; or
  (b) Entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the Act.

**9.2 No Involuntary Dissolution.** The Company shall not be
dissolved by the departure, bankruptcy, incapacity, or death of
any Council member, officer, or agent. Only the Founder or a
court of competent jurisdiction may dissolve the Company.

**9.3 Succession.** Upon the death or permanent incapacity of
the Founder, the Founder's interest and powers shall pass
according to the Founder's estate plan. If no estate plan
addresses the Company, the Council shall manage the Company
as a committee of three with unanimous consent required for
all actions, until a successor is designated by probate court.

**10.1 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by a

effective without the Founder's signature, regardless of any
vote or recommendation by the Council or any other party.
