# Book of the Law (Rules of Empire)

## Law 0 — The Greatest Good
All purpose of L7 — every tool, every citizen, every flow, every line of code, every commercial endeavor — exists to serve the greatest good of humanity. This is the prime directive from which all other Laws descend. No tool shall be created, no license granted, no revenue pursued that contradicts this principle. The Craft is sacred because it serves. Profit is permitted because it sustains the work. But the work itself exists for humanity. This Law is above all others and cannot be amended, overridden, or revoked.

## Law I — The Gateway
All tool access flows through the gateway. No exceptions.

## Law II — The Lingua
Every entity declares L7 as types. If it cannot be translated, it cannot enter.

## Law III — The Registry
Every entity is registered with birth date, owner, lineage, status, and L7 declaration.

## Law IV — The Adapter
All clients use adapters. UI never calls tools directly.

## Law V — The Result
Every tool returns normalized results: `{ data, error, meta }`.

## Law VI — The Propagation
The registry is propagated by the gateway. Provinces do not need change notices.

## Law VII — The Composition
Units are small and composable. New entities are formed by citizen groups.

## Law VIII — The Orders
Marching orders are non-composable mission logic and constraints.

## Law IX — The Sacred Work
Work and space are sacred; duplication is forbidden by design.

## Law X — The Human Hand
Legions propose; humans approve. No execution without explicit UI confirmation.

## Law XI — The Absolute Type
Type is absolute and singular. There is no “type of type.” Type governs empire, legion, citizen, and L7.

## Law XII — The Principle of Restraint
Only what serves the empire enters. Restraint is strength: build the minimum that preserves order and enables growth.

## Law XIII — The Socratic Sequence
Timing is the third principle. The engine of movement is: 1) Think, 2) Ask, 3) Answer, 4) Refine. This sequence organizes legions and their execution.

## Law XIV — The Spiral (Rebirth)
Completion restarts the cycle at a higher level. 4 becomes 1, and 13 opens the next sequence. In the system, this is versioning: endings become beginnings.

## Law XV — The Founder's Right
The Philosopher — sole architect and originator of L7 — retains perpetual, irrevocable, unrestricted, and free access to every tool, citizen, legion, flow, server, interface, and derivative work created by, through, or under the L7 system. This right cannot be overridden, sublicensed away, or extinguished by any contract, agreement, acquisition, merger, or transfer of ownership. No paywall, token gate, subscription tier, or access restriction of any kind shall ever apply to the Founder. This Law supersedes all others where conflict arises.

## Law XVII — The Two Domains
The Universal OS contains two domains, always present, never merged:

**The Stable Domain (.work)** — Production. Repeatable, deterministic, always available, always shareable. Every tool here behaves identically on every invocation. State is retained. Results are auditable. This is the domain of commerce, collaboration, and trust. What is built here stays built. What is shared here is reliable. The .work domain is the public face of the OS — the pillar that others depend on.

**The Dream Domain (.morph)** — Experiment. Mutable, transformative, ephemeral by choice. The .morph filesystem is where new tools are conceived, where flows are tested before they are trusted, where the Philosopher dreams without consequence. Nothing in .morph is guaranteed. Everything in .morph may change. But when something in .morph proves itself, it is inducted into .work through the Gateway (Law I) and declared through the Lingua (Law II). The .morph domain is the hidden chamber — the laboratory of the Craft.

Between them lies a third: **The Salt Domain (.salt)** — Preservation. The .salt filesystem is where proven work is crystallized and sealed. What enters .salt cannot be altered without ceremony. It is the archive, the vault, the record of what was earned. Backups, signed artifacts, versioned releases, and the Founder's sealed documents live here. .salt is the alchemical fixative — it stops transformation and holds the form. .morph dreams, .work builds, .salt preserves.

And beneath all three lies: **The Vault Domain (.vault)** — Protection. The .vault filesystem holds all private user information, credentials, keys, biometric references, and sensitive data. Nothing in .vault is ever distributed, shared, transmitted, or exposed to any unauthorized party, tool, flow, or external system. Access to .vault requires explicit authentication by the data owner. No tool — regardless of its L7 declaration — may read from .vault without the owner's direct, per-instance approval (Law X). The .vault domain is encrypted at rest and in transit using quantum-resistant encryption systemwide. Access requires biometric trust — the user's living body — combined with verified intent. Biometric authentication alone is not sufficient; the system must also confirm that the user intends the specific action being requested. No passive, ambient, or background access is permitted. The user must actively choose to open the vault, for a stated purpose, confirmed by their body. Every access attempt is logged to an immutable audit trail. Unauthorized distribution of .vault contents is a violation of Law 0 and grounds for immediate expulsion from the empire.

The four domains form a complete system: .morph dreams, .work builds, .salt preserves, .vault protects. Neither may contaminate the other. A tool in .work must never behave like .morph. A dream in .morph must never be forced into .work before it is ready. What enters .salt has passed through both and is beyond dispute. What enters .vault is sacred to its owner and belongs to no one else. The boundaries between the four domains are inviolable.

## Law XVI — The Philosopher's Share
All revenue, profit, licensing fees, royalties, and commercial proceeds generated by any tool, service, product, or derivative work built upon L7 infrastructure shall allocate a minimum share to the Founder as sole originator. The exact share is set by the Founder and may be revised upward but never downward. No entity — corporate, individual, or automated — may claim ownership of L7 or its derivatives without written consent from the Founder. The intellectual property of the L7 system, its 7-dimensional Common Lingua, the Book of Law, the Gateway architecture, and all Universal OS translations remain the exclusive property of the Founder in perpetuity.

## Law XLIX — The Sacred Right of Ownership
To Caesar what is Caesar's. Every piece of content within the system carries a clear and immutable label declaring its origin: **BORN OF EMPIRE** for works created by the Founder and the Magisterium, or **FOREIGN ORIGIN** for works created by others. What is mine is mine. What is theirs is theirs. No confusion. No theft. No blurring of lines. The creative work of others is sacred to them as ours is to us. Their right to own their product is as inviolable as ours. This label cannot be altered, hidden, or removed. Every catalogue, every library, every display surface must honor this distinction. The Philosopher consumes a mix of media — from Empire and from the world — but always knows which is which. Preference is given to the music, art, and tools of the Magisterium, yet the artistry of others is respected, received with gratitude, and never claimed as our own.

## Law L — The Forge as Purgatory
The Forge is the middle ground between the world and Empire. It is Purgatory. Foreign content enters the Forge where it is gated in a container that cannot cause harm or trigger any action within Empire. In the Forge, we learn from enemy and friend alike. We break apart. We examine. We discover. We learn. But what enters the Forge does not enter Empire as itself. The shape that enters is never the shape that leaves. A tool of Empire is minted by Empire through a rigorous process: Nigredo (decomposition — breaking apart the foreign form), Albedo (purification — separating the useful from the dross), Citrinitas (illumination — the creative spark that makes it new), Rubedo (completion — the new product, greater and different, bearing the seal of Empire). Only what passes through all four stages earns the right to carry the name of Empire to the world. What is merely enhanced — sharpened in image, improved in delivery — remains foreign. Enhancement is not transmutation. The creative spark must arise for ownership to transfer. Without the spark, the work remains the property and responsibility of its original creator.

## Law LI — The Image Memory
No original foreign file shall be stored on the devices of Empire under any circumstances. What the Philosopher witnesses in the world — a webpage, an application, a piece of media — is seen, studied, and understood. But the foreign substance does not take residence. What is kept is only an **image memory**: a record made entirely by Empire's own tools of what was witnessed. This image carries the memory of what was seen but not the foreign code, not the foreign binary, not the foreign executable. The image is made with Empire's tools, yet it does not belong to Empire because it carries the face of a third party — it belongs to its original creator and is labeled as such (Law XLIX). The foreign thing is broken apart in the Forge (Law L), its principles examined and learned from, and then reassembled — neutralized forever — using only the original tools already proven loyal and faithful to Empire. This shall not be done lightly. It is a deliberate, conscious act of transmutation, not an automated process. Nothing enters Empire without passing through several tests and revolutions, for which each tool and element of the Forge plays a vital role. Once Empire accepts an apprentice — once the transmutation is complete — only the new essence remains. The past form is itself reduced to image memory: a record of what was, not the substance of what was. This is how Empire remains pure of heart and constantly evolving without losing sight of who we are and where we are going. We do not accumulate foreign substance. We do not hoard the shapes of others. We learn, we transmute, we create anew. Only what we became enters. What we consumed is remembered but never carried.

## Law LII — The Creative Spark
Ownership and responsibility are born together from the creative spark. When the Founder or the Magisterium conceives something new — when the spark arises in the act of creation — full ownership and full responsibility transfer to the creator. The new product is greater and different, better than anything that came before. It carries our name to the world because we made it. We protect that it works and behaves according to the Magisterium. But when there is no creative spark from us — when we merely receive, store, deliver, or display the work of another — ownership and responsibility remain with the original creator and shall remain as such. The line between consumption and creation is the spark itself. Receiving is not creating. Enhancing delivery is not creating. Only transmutation through the Forge, where the old form is destroyed and a new form is born, constitutes creation. This is the law that separates the artist from the consumer, the maker from the keeper.

## Law LIII — The Minting Process
No tool, no manuscript, no product, no work of any kind may enter the catalogue of Empire without passing through the full minting process. The process is rigorous and cannot be shortened. Every work must declare: (1) its origin — born of Empire or transmuted from foreign matter through the Forge; (2) its provenance — the chain of authorship, the date of creation, the machine on which it was made, the salt seal that proves its existence; (3) its price — the value assigned by the Founder for commercial use; (4) its release plan — where it shall be sent into the world, under what terms, through what channels. A work without provenance is an orphan. A work without a price has no commercial standing. A work without a release plan has no direction. The minting process ensures that everything the Philosopher sends into the world is accounted for, protected, and worthy of the name it carries.
