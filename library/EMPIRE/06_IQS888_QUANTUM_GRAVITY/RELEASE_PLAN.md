## Submission Strategy

### Primary Target: Mathematical Physics Preprint
1. **arXiv (hep-th or gr-qc)**
   - Immediate global distribution, establishes priority date
   - Free submission
   - Category: hep-th (High Energy Physics — Theory) or gr-qc (General Relativity and Quantum Cosmology)
   - Note: arXiv requires endorsement for first-time submitters in physics categories

2. **viXra** (backup if arXiv endorsement unavailable)
   - No endorsement required
   - Less prestige but establishes timestamp

### Secondary Target: Journals
3. **Foundations of Physics (Springer)**
   - Interdisciplinary physics foundations journal
   - Accepts unconventional but rigorous mathematical frameworks
   - Impact Factor: ~1.5

4. **Classical and Quantum Gravity (IOP)**
   - Dedicated to gravitational physics
   - Strong for mathematical treatments

5. **Journal of Mathematical Physics (AIP)**
   - Mathematical rigor expected — good fit for derivation-heavy paper

6. **International Journal of Modern Physics D (World Scientific)**
   - Gravitation, astrophysics, cosmology
   - More receptive to novel frameworks

### Tertiary Target: Open Access
7. **Universe (MDPI)** — Open access, rapid review
8. **Symmetry (MDPI)** — If emphasizing the hexagrammatic symmetry group

## Pre-Submission Steps
1. Create GitHub repository: github.com/avalia1/IQS888

3. Seek arXiv endorser (physics faculty contact at UNC or elsewhere)
4. Prepare cover letter emphasizing mathematical novelty
5. Identify 3-5 potential referees working in quantum gravity foundations
