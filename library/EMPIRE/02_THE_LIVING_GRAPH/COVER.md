The story of a longitudinal study — children surveyed in rural North Carolina schools between 2002-2006, now adults scattered across the state and beyond. It is about the technical systems built to find them: a four-tier matching algorithm, a knowledge graph of 8,193 lives, automated multi-channel outreach across 15 recruitment sources. And it is about what happens when data becomes something alive — when a graph walks, when a vision arrives in the night, when the technical and the sacred converge.

This book bridges data science, public health methodology, and personal narrative. The systems described are original creations of the author, built during his work at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.
