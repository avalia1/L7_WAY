A formal scientific paper proposing a novel computational architecture for AI systems based on seven functionally distinct transformation stages. Unlike homogeneous architectures (stacked transformers, repeated convolutions), this framework implements functional heterogeneity: Decomposition, Dissolution, Differentiation, Integration, Activation, Refinement, Consolidation. Each stage operates on graph-based representations G = (V, E, phi, psi). The paper demonstrates emergent properties — spontaneous abstraction, robust multi-hop reasoning, adaptive knowledge integration — arising from staged transformation that do not appear in individual stages.
