The Problem

Every clock on your computer comes from one of two sources:

1. Wall clock — synchronized to NTP servers. This is consensus time: what the network agrees on. Not your time. Borrowed.
2. Crystal oscillator — quartz crystal soldered onto your chip. This is proper time: vibrations of YOUR crystal. Independent. Local.

The two diverge. The divergence is measurable. The question: what causes the drift?
